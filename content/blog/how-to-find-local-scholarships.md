## The Secret Hiding in Plain Sight

Every state has dozens of community foundations quietly giving away scholarship money each year. Unlike national awards that attract thousands of applicants, these local funds often receive fewer than 50 applications.

The reason? Nobody knows about them.

## What Is a Community Foundation?

A community foundation is a nonprofit that pools donations from local families, businesses, and estates to give back to the community. Most have scholarship funds specifically for students in their county or city.

They're IRS-registered, file public 990 tax returns, and have been operating for decades. They're not scams — they're just undermarketed.

## Why Local Beats National

**National scholarship:** 10,000 applicants, $5,000 prize. Your odds: 0.01%.

**Local community foundation:** 30 applicants, $2,500 prize. Your odds: 3%.

Even though the dollar amount is lower, your expected value is dramatically higher with local awards.

## How to Find Them

- Search "[your county] community foundation scholarships"
- Search "[your city] foundation scholarship fund"
- Check your high school guidance office

## What They Look For

Most community foundations care about local ties, community involvement, financial need, and essay quality. With few applicants, a well-written essay stands out dramatically.

## The Bottom Line

If you live in a mid-sized city or rural area, there are almost certainly 3-5 local foundation scholarships you qualify for right now. The applications take an afternoon. The competition is low. The money is real.
